Description

Tiramisu is one of Italy's most beloved desserts, made with layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cream, and a dusting of cocoa powder.

Light, creamy, and full of rich coffee flavor, tiramisu is an elegant dessert that requires no baking and tastes even better after chilling overnight.

Ingredients

  • 250 g mascarpone cheese
  • 200 ml heavy cream
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 50 g sugar
  • 1 cup strong espresso
  • Ladyfinger biscuits
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Steps

  1. Brew strong espresso and allow it to cool.
  2. Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale.
  3. Fold in mascarpone cheese.
  4. Whip the heavy cream and gently combine it with the mascarpone mixture.
  5. Dip each ladyfinger briefly in the espresso.
  6. Arrange a layer of ladyfingers in a dish.
  7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the biscuits.
  8. Repeat the layers.
  9. Dust generously with cocoa powder.
  10. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours before serving.
